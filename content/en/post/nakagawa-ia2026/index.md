On March 3, 2026, Kota Nakagawa from our laboratory presented his research at [IEICE Technical Committee on Internet Architecture (IA)](https://ken.ieice.org/ken/program/index.php?mode=program&tgs_regid=1705f6c1f4d9b098dacd43164a48c997eba065ae60b780989aaf68c077a10b19&tgid=IEICE-IA&layout=&lang=eng).

He introduced his research titled ["Proposal of Low-Latency Source Routing Method Using Comprehensive Path Discovery via INT Packet Replication"](https://ken.ieice.org/ken/paper/20260303fcSU/eng/), which proposed a method for selecting low-latency paths that are not constrained by the minimum number of hops, based on latency measurement utilizing In-band Network Telemetry and SRv6 source routing. For this work, he received the Student Research Encouragement Award.
